Technology Integration Lesson Plan

Standards and Essental Questions

PA Core Standards: CC.1.4.8.G, CC1.4.8.H, CC.1.4.8.I, CC.1.4.8.J, CC.1.4.8.K, CC1.4.8.L

ISTE Standards: 1c, 2c, 3a, 3b, 3c, 3d, 7b, 7c

Essential Questions:

  • What is the difference between persuasive and argument writing?
  • How should in-text citations be formatted? Why do I need to use them?
  • What is plagiarism and why is it important to avoid it?
  • How do I research, evaluate sources, and take notes to use later?
  • How do I plan an argument essay built on appropriate and cited evidence?
  • How do I draft an argument essay using appropriate formal tone, evidence, and a counter argument?
  • How do I write to effectively argue and persuade using a formal tone?
  • How do I edit for conventions of language?

Teaching

Activator: Think, pair, share: Think of a time you lost an argument with your responsible adult. What could you have differently to be more persuasive?

Teaching:

  1. Schoology is utilized as the LMS. Assignments are posted with Google Docs attached which automatically makes and saves a copy for each student. The teacher retains final control over all documents created this way including over the sharing settings. Rubric grading occurs on Schoology and integrates with PowerGrade so that student grades are automatically updated.
  2. Class is conducted according to a station rotation model with students spending time either independently, collaboratively, or in direct instruction. The daily agenda is posted on a Google Doc displaying in Schoology. Students are able to see all previous daily agendas.
  3. Writing and literature are developed in separate units but worked on at the same time.
  4. Steps of the writing process are controlled through the ‘assignment completion’ feature so that students can’t move on to one step until the previous meets the minimum acceptable score.
